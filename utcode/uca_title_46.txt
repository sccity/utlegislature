Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-101 Title.
-------------------------------------
This chapter is known as the "Uniform Electronic Transactions Act."

Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102 Definitions.
-------------------------------------------
As used in this chapter:Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(1): "Agreement" means the bargain of the parties in fact, as found in their language or inferred from other circumstances and from rules, regulations, and procedures given the effect of agreements under laws otherwise applicable to a particular transaction.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(2): "Automated transaction" means a transaction conducted or performed, in whole or in part, by electronic means or electronic records, in which the acts or records of one or both parties are not reviewed by an individual in the ordinary course in forming a contract, performing under an existing contract, or fulfilling an obligation required by the transaction.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(3): "Computer program" means a set of statements or instructions to be used directly or indirectly in an information processing system in order to bring about a certain result.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(4): "Contract" means the total legal obligation resulting from the parties' agreement as affected by this chapter and other applicable law.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(5): "Electronic" means relating to technology having electrical, digital, magnetic, wireless, optical, electromagnetic, or similar capabilities.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(6): "Electronic agent" means a computer program or an electronic or other automated means used independently to initiate an action or respond to electronic records or performances in whole or in part, without review or action by an individual.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(7): "Electronic record" means a record created, generated, sent, communicated, received, or stored by electronic means.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(8): "Electronic signature" means an electronic sound, symbol, or process attached to or logically associated with a record and executed or adopted by a person with the intent to sign the record.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(9): "Governmental agency" means an executive, legislative, or judicial agency, department, board, commission, authority, institution, or instrumentality of the federal government or of a state or of a county, municipality, or other political subdivision of a state.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(10): "Information" means data, text, images, sounds, codes, computer programs, software, databases, or the like.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(11): "Information processing system" means an electronic system for creating, generating, sending, receiving, storing, displaying, or processing information.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(12): "Person" means an individual, corporation, business trust, estate, trust, partnership, limited liability company, association, joint venture, governmental agency, public corporation, or any other legal or commercial entity.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(13): "Record" means information that is inscribed on a tangible medium or that is stored in an electronic or other medium and is retrievable in perceivable form.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(14): 
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(14)(a): "Security procedure" means a procedure employed for the purpose of verifying that an electronic signature, record, or performance is that of a specific person or for detecting changes or errors in the information in an electronic record.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(14)(b): "Security procedure" includes a procedure that requires the use of algorithms or other codes, identifying words or numbers, encryption, or callback or other acknowledgment procedures.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(15): 
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(15)(a): "State" means a state of the United States, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, the United States Virgin Islands, or any territory or insular possession subject to the jurisdiction of the United States.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(15)(b): "State" includes an Indian tribe or band, or Alaskan native village, that is recognized by federal law or formally acknowledged by a state.
Utah Code Annotated § 46-4-102(16): "Transaction" means an action or set of actions occurring between two or more persons relating to the conduct of business, commercial, or governmental affairs.

Utah Code Annotated § 46-5-110 Uniformity of application and construction.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
In applying and construing this uniform act, consideration must be given to the need to promote uniformity of the law with respect to its subject matter among states that enact it.

Utah Code Annotated § 46-5-111 Relation to Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This chapter modifies, limits, and supersedes the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act, 15 U.S.C. Sec. 7001 et seq., but does not modify, limit, or supersede Section 101(c) of that act, 15 U.S.C. Sec. 7001(c), or authorize electronic delivery of any of the notices described in Section 103(b) of that act, 15 U.S.C. Sec. 7003(b).

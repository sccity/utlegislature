Utah Code Annotated - Title 64 - Chapter 9b - Work Programs for Prisoners
*************************************************************************

Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1 Legislative findings.
---------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(1): The Legislature finds that it is in the best interest of the state for the department to:
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(1)(a): develop job opportunities to further enhance the rehabilitation of inmates of the Utah state prison;
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(1)(b): establish and actively work toward the goal that all inmates shall be productively involved in a treatment, education, or work program, or a combination of these programs, as appropriate, except for inmates who the department determines have a physical or mental disability, or pose a danger to the public, so that they are unable to engage in these activities; and
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(1)(c): submit a comprehensive management plan outlining the department's plan to meet this goal to the Legislature on or before November 1 of each even-numbered year, and the plan shall include:
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(1)(c)(i): a cost-effective analysis of current inmate education, treatment, and work programs; and
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(1)(c)(ii): a study of the feasibility of expanding inmate work programs, particularly in regard to programs that:
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(1)(c)(ii)(A): are not capital intensive;
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(1)(c)(ii)(B): do not unfairly compete with existing Utah industry; and
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(1)(c)(ii)(C): are designed to increase the motivation, develop the work capabilities, and foster the cooperation of inmates.
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-1(2): The Legislature further finds that a proper means to accomplish this is through a liberal application of this chapter.


Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-2 Definitions.
------------------------------------------
As used in this chapter:Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-2(1): "Department" means the Department of Corrections.
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-2(2): "Inmate" means any man or woman who is under the jurisdiction of the department and who is assigned to the Utah state prison or to a county jail.


Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-3 Encouragement of private industry -- Types of employers to be sought.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-3(1): The department is authorized to encourage private industry to locate and provide rehabilitative and job opportunities for inmates at the Utah state prison and county jails housing inmates under the jurisdiction of the department.
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-3(2): The department shall determine what type of employer is to be allowed to locate at the prison or county jail, taking into consideration the physical facilities and space at the prison or county jail, the abilities of the inmates, and the type of product to be produced by the employer.


Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-4 Work to be voluntary -- Payment of prevailing wages.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-4(1): Rehabilitative and job opportunities at the Utah state prison and participating county jails shall not be forced upon any inmate contrary to the
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-4(2): Private businesses that manufacture products for sale in Utah or in interstate commerce shall pay inmates the prevailing wage for similar work in local private industry.  Private businesses that provide services, agricultural products, or manufactured products for export shall pay inmates wages determined by the department, but should not displace local Utah workers as a result of their employment of inmates.


Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-5 Intent of Legislature.
----------------------------------------------------
It is the legislative intent, and inmates are encouraged, to use their personal earnings from jobs created under this chapter for the following:Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-5(1): for restitution to the victims of the inmate's criminal offense, where applicable;
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-5(2): for support of the inmate's family, where applicable;
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-5(3): for the inmate's personal use; and
Utah Code Annotated § 64-9b-5(4): for reimbursement of security, operational, and other costs incurred by the Utah Correctional Industries Division of the department in administering these projects.

----------------------------------------------
The executive director shall appoint deputy directors, wardens, regional administrators, and other administrators as necessary to administer correctional programs. Utah Code Annotated - Title 64 - Chapter 13a - Utah Correctional Industries Act
*******************************************************************************

Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-1 Title.
-------------------------------------
This act is known as the "Utah Correctional Industries Act."

Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-2 Legislative intent.
--------------------------------------------------
It is the intent of the Legislature in this chapter to:Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-2(1): create a Division of Correctional Industries which:
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-2(1)(a): is a self-supporting organization;
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-2(1)(b): is profit-oriented;
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-2(1)(c): generates revenue for its operations and capital investment; and
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-2(1)(d): assumes responsibility for training offenders in general work habits, work skills, and specific training skills that increase their employment prospects when released;
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-2(2): provide an environment for the operation of correctional industries that closely resembles the environment for the business operations of a private corporate entity;
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-2(3): make the Division of Correctional Industries responsible for and accountable to the Legislature and to the governor for correctional industries programs in this state.


Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-3 Definitions.
-------------------------------------------
As used in this chapter "division" means the Division of Correctional Industries.

Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-4 Creation of division -- Authority of director.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
There is created in the Department of Corrections the Division of Correctional Industries, which shall be under the direction of the director of correctional industries, who shall be appointed by the director of the Department of Corrections.  The director of correctional industries shall have considerable business operations experience, including the supervision and management of production operations, and has the authority to:Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-4(1): determine the personnel needs and requirements of the program;
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-4(2): hire all subordinate personnel in accordance with state policy and procedures; and
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-4(3): market and deliver correctional industry products and services.


Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-7 Prison industry properties belong to state.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
All money received by prison industries in the administration of this chapter, except as otherwise provided, together with all property acquired, administered, possessed, or received by prison industries are the property of the state, and all expenses, debts, and liabilities incurred by prison industries in connection with the administration of this chapter shall be paid from the Utah Correctional Industries Fund.

Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-8 Reports to governor.
---------------------------------------------------
Utah correctional industries shall make reports to the governor concerning the administration of this chapter, as the governor may require.

Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-9 Expenses to be paid from fund.
-------------------------------------------------------------
All expenditures necessary for the administration of this chapter, including the payment of all salaries, necessary overhead expenses, acquisition of assets, and raw materials shall be paid by warrants of the state drawn on the state treasurer to be paid out of the Utah Correctional Industries Fund.

Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-10 Payment of accounts.
----------------------------------------------------
All accounts payable by Utah correctional industries shall be preaudited by a person designated by the Utah Correctional Industries Division. Requisitions for payment of accounts shall be signed and countersigned by persons designated by the Utah Correctional Industries Division.

Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-11 Financial statements.
-----------------------------------------------------
The financial statements of the Utah Correctional Industries Division shall be made up quarterly to the last day of September, December, March, and June in each year, and at other times as determined by the governor, and in every case Utah correctional industries shall prepare a balance sheet, income statement, and statement of changes in financial position.

Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-12 Audit of financial statements.
--------------------------------------------------------------
The financial statements of the fund may be audited annually by the state auditor or by another person, firm, or corporation the state auditor may appoint.

Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-13 Purchases of material -- Exemption.
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-13(1): Title 63G, Chapter 6a, Utah Procurement Code The Division of Correctional Industries is exempt from the provisions of
Utah Code Annotated § 64-13a-13(2): The purchase of raw materials for use by the division in manufacturing or processing products for resale is exempt from the powers and duties of the state purchasing agent.

-----------------------------------------
